Active vs. Passive Voice

It's all about focus. Does the sentence focus on who is doing the action, or who is receiving it? Let's follow the arrows.

The "Doer" Focus

Active Voice

Subject (The Doer)
Verb
Object (Receiver)
Formula
Subject + Verb + Object

The boy kicked the ball.

The "Receiver" Focus

Passive Voice

Subject
(Old Object)
Be + V3
Agent
(by...)
Formula
Object + "Be" + V3 + by Subject

The ball was kicked by the boy.

The Structural "Swap"

The chef cooked the meal.
The meal was cooked by the chef.

When should I use the Passive Voice?

1

The Doer is Unknown

You don't know who did the action.

"My wallet was stolen!"

2

The Doer is Obvious

We already know who normally does it.

"The thief was arrested."
(Obviously, by the police)

3

The Object is More Important

You want to highlight the receiver, not the creator.

"The Mona Lisa was painted in 1503."
